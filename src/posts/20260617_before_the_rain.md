title: before the rain
tags: musings
date: 2026-06-17
description: .
---

on a quiet autumn day,
i rest on my steering wheel, melancholy,
as youth chatter through the bus window.  

just as i realize there’s no going back,
i blink,
and there i am.
i burst into tears. 
they turn to me quizzically and, embarrassed, 
I hide my face;
for at twelve, 
there are no words to express the realization 
of the ephemeral nature and weightlessness of youth,
for youth is all we’ve had. 
the burdens of our parents
are not yet our own,
and the temperament of the world is yet shielded from view

the feeling dissipates as we tumble off,
basking in the breeze in our hair,
in the freedom of education,
from education.
the bus moves on,
but the waiting line hesitates
as the leading car sighs:
“wasted”

how are we expected to cherish what we’ve always had?
memories of sun stretch infinitely back,
and though clouds loom ahead,
we’ve never felt the rain.

i blink
and it’s passed.
as she gathers leaves
and tosses them into the air,
i feel protective.
let her believe in the magic of the world.
time and knowledge march infinitely forward.
her time is now
